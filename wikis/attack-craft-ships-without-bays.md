Not every starship has dedicated [Launch Bays](launch-bays-overview.md) to host whole [Squadrons](squadrons-overview.md) of [Attack Craft](attack-craft-rules.md) and shuttles. However, most starships have some sort of landing bay with room for shuttles and [Small Craft](attack-craft-small-craft.md). Generally, Imperial ships are too large to easily land on a planet's surface, and small craft become vital for loading crew and cargo, and resupplying the vessel.

The [Size](character-traits.md) of these small bays can vary wildly based on a ship's size and class. However, if GMs or players wish to determine how many shuttles a ship may have room for, a useful guideline is that a ship has room for one lighter, shuttle, or heavy lifter for every 5 points of Space its [Hull](starship-anatomy-detailed.md) has. In general, these vehicles shouldn't be larger than 50 metres. However, if a ship has a cargo hold component (such as the [Cargo Hold and Lighter Bay](starship-supplemental-components.md)), they should be able to hold an additional four vehicles.

Most  [Attack](combat-attack-rules.md)  craft  operations  (see  page  12)  begin  the operation by launching attack craft squadrons currently ready in  the  launch  bays.  Once  a  bay  has  launched  squadrons, additional squadrons can be readied for operations-so long as  the  number  of  squadrons  readied  does  not  exceed  the Strength  of  the  Launch  Bay.  These  squadrons  can  even  be launched on subsequent turns. Needless to say, a ship cannot launch more squadrons than it possesses.
