[Squadrons](squadrons-overview.md) are composed of two or more vessels of similar [Size](character-traits.md).  Under normal circumstances, only vessels of the same [Hull](starship-anatomy-detailed.md)  type  can  be  grouped  into  a  squadron.  In  some  cases, however, it may be desirable to group [Frigates](hulls-overview.md) and [Raiders](ships-raiders-overview.md) into the  same  squadron,  or  to  group  cruisers  and  [Battlecruisers](ships-battlecruisers-overview.md) or  grand  cruisers.  In  broad  terms,  the  different  tendencies towards speed, structure and armament of a given hull type may make combining different hull types difficult in practice, particularly when the hulls are very different.

Beyond that, the way that squadrons carry out actions means that wildly different armament can cause problems as well-a squadron with only one torpedo-armed vessel will not fully benefit from spending actions to [Reload](rules-combat-overview.md) those [Torpedoes](weapons-torpedoes.md), as the

action is wasted for every vessel but the torpedo-armed one.

Typically,  squadrons  may  be  compiled  of  [Transports](ships-transports-overview.md), raiders and frigates, [Light Cruisers](ships-light-cruisers-overview.md), cruisers, and battlecruisers, or [Grand Cruisers](ships-grand-cruisers-overview.md). However, at the GM's discretion, different

hull types can be mixed in squadrons.



*Source:* `Battle Fleet of the Koronus, page 114`
