N ot all goods and services are of the same quality. In the  vastness  of  the  Imperium  and  myriad  worlds beyond, the Craftsmanship of items can vary widely-everything  from  crude  mass-produced  materials of  Poor Craftsmanship to handcrafted masterpieces of Best Craftsmanship can be found for sale. This can especially be the case in a frontier area such as the Koronus Expanse, where there are few established industrial centres, and as such many [Weapons](weapons-general.md) are unlikely to be native to the Expanse. Some of these imports may be centuries-old products of The Calixis Sector and have passed through many hands. Similarly, those who offer services might be lazy and unskilled at their trade if  of  Poor Craftsmanship, or may be the most skilled craftsman of an entire world if of Best Craftsmanship. Unless specifically stated, the Craftsmanship of any object or retainer providing a service is considered Common.

Items  of  Poor  Craftsmanship  are  generally  more  [Prone](combat-special-circumstances.md) to  failure  and  breakage,  while  items  of  Good  and  Best Craftsmanship are more durable, though it is up to the GM how and when these effects might enter play. In the case of weaponry and [Armour](armour.md), effects on an item's performance or weight are detailed later in this chapter, but for other goods differences  of  quality  are  merely  descriptive.  The  GM  may reduce the Difficulty of some Tests involving items of better Craftsmanship, but that is entirely at his discretion.

Services are more affected than goods by the Craftsmanship of  the  retainers  who  provide  them.  Craftsmanship  can determine skill levels, willingness to toil, and loyalty of the retainers, as well as other aspects at the choice of the GM.

Craftsmanship  also  affects  the  time  taken  to  find  goods or services and complete an [Acquisition](economy-acquisition-rules.md). Table 5-3: Craftsmanship and Time Taken provides a multiplier for the time to make an [Acquisition](economy-acquisition-rules.md) determined in Table 5-2: [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md), Population, and Time .
