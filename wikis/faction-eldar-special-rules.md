All Eldar ships are subject to the following [Special Rules](mass-combat-special-rules.md): Master Starfarers: [The Eldar](faction-eldar-overview.md) are some of the most skilled voidfarers in existence and their ships are designed to make the most of their talents. Any Eldar aboard an Eldar vessel may re-roll any Piloting Tests for [Manoeuvre Actions](starship-combat-rules.md).

Path  of  the  Warrior: When  a  Craftworld  Eldar  vessel engages in a boarding action, it doesn't send mere [Ratings](crew-ratings.md) into the fray. Instead, well trained and well armed Eldar Aspect Warriors will lead [The Attack](rules-combat-overview.md) (or defence). Thus, Craftworld Eldar receive +10 on any Command Tests made as part of a Boarding Action and inflict 1d5+2 Crew Population and 1d5+2 Morale [Damage](character-injury.md) to enemy ships (representing the ease with which Aspect Warriors slaughter their foes).

Limited Crew: The Eldar are not a numerous people and the [Size](character-traits.md) of their ship's crews reflect this. In addition, many Eldar vessels are controlled in part (or whole) by an Infinity Circuit containing the souls of dead Eldar, further reducing crew [Requirements](economy-endeavours.md). All Eldar Crew Population losses are increased by one.

Dedicated Crew: Any  given  Eldar  has  seen  centuries or  even  millennia  pass  by .  Thus,  they  are  highly proficient in their duties and difficult to rattle. Any time an Eldar ships loses Crew Morale, it loses 1 less point of Morale (to a minimum of 1).Sailing On The Solar Winds: Ships equipped with Solar Sails  subtract  one  from  their  Speed  value  if  they  begin  a Strategic  Turn  moving  towards  the  nearest  sun,  use  listed speeds while [Flying](combat-movement.md) away, and add one to their Speed value when moving at a (roughly) right angle to it. However, these modifications only apply within a solar system and can be ignored if the vessel is in deep space.

Supreme  Manoeuvrability: A  ship  with  Solar  Sails  may interrupt  its  Manoeuvre  Action  at  any  point  to  perform  a Shooting Action. Once the Shooting Action is resolved, it must complete the remainder of its Manoeuvre Action. The limit of one Shooting Action per turn still applies. In addition, all Eldar vessels can make turns of 90 degrees, regardless of [Hull](starship-anatomy-detailed.md) size.

Holographic  Cloaking: All  attacks  made  against  a  ship with a functioning Holofield suffer -40 to any Test to hit in addition to any other penalties (for [Example](rules-tests.md), this would include both a Ballistic Skill Test to hit the ship with [Lances](starship-supplemental-components.md), and a Pilot (Space Craft) + Manoeuvrability Test to ram the  Holofield  equipped  ship).  Macrobatteries,  due  to  their massive  broadsides  capable  of  filling  a  large  area  of  space with ordinance, only suffer a -20 to hit. Ships also suffer a -30 to any attempts to use any Extended Actions against the a  holofielded ship that involve Detection (such as Lock on Target and Focused Augury).

Eldar [Defensive](weapons-general.md) Doctrine: Eldar ships have no [Void Shields](components-void-shields.md), and always have their Holofield active unless it is destroyed.
