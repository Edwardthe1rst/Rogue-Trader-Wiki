A soldier is  only  as  effective  as  the  equipment  he's  issued. While some military scholars will argue otherwise, claiming that  superior  training  can  overcome  inferior  equipment, it  doesn't  matter  how  good  an  infantryman's  training  has been if his [Lasgun](weapons-general.md) won't shoot and his [Armour](armour.md) is in tatters. Unfortunately, [Sacrifices](psychic-psyker-types.md) often need to  be  made  when outfitting large units, and soldiers usually have to make do with  what  they've  been  given.  Soldiers'  equipment  comes in three qualities: Poor, Common, and Good. These [Ratings](crew-ratings.md) are  identical  to  the  [Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md)  ratings  in  Table  9-35: [Acquisition](economy-acquisition-rules.md)  Modifiers  on  page  272  of  the Rogue TRadeR core  rulebook,  and  have  the  same  Acquisition  Modifiers. It's a delicate balance outfitting troops. Good quality [Gear](equipment-gear.md) is expensive but keeps troops happy.
