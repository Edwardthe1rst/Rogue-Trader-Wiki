## Corruption Points

Corruption Points (CP) operate almost exactly like Insanity Points, except that they are gained through exposure to [The Warp](warp-imperial-space-travel.md), dark rituals, cursed artefacts, and [Daemonic](character-traits.md) [Influence](economy-influence-rules.md). The  more  Corruption  Points  a  character  has,  the  more afflicted he becomes; this is reflected in the rules by the risk of  Malignancies  and  [Mutation](character-mutations-list.md)  as  shown  on Table  10-7: The Corruption Track .

### Chaos and the Common Citizen

The rules for Corruption presented here are for use with Player  Characters;  Rogue  Traders  and  their  followers are men and women of destiny and purpose-exactly the  sort  of  people  that  the  Ruinous  Powers  seek  to slowly  corrupt.  For  most,  the  touch  of  Chaos  brings summary destruction, madness, [Mutation](character-mutations-list.md), and death.

Corruption Points inflicted on him are equal to the being's [Fear](character-fear-and-damnation.md) Rating score (i.e. Frightening 2 equals 2 CP).

### Rending the Veil

Characters caught in a full-blown intrusion of [The Warp](warp-imperial-space-travel.md) into corporeal reality gain Corruption Points from the experience. Just how many depends on what occurs and the severity of the breach. This might be anywhere from a single point to several d10s in value.

### Sorcery

The practice of sorcery, witnessing dread rituals, and invoking daemons are all causes of Corruption, regardless of the reason. Normally minor rituals cause 1d10 Corruption Points, and major ceremonies can cause many more.

### Blasphemous Lore

Knowledge  itself  can  corrupt,  and  the  study  of  certain tomes, pict-logs, and even some debased artwork can cause Corruption in the viewer.

### Dark Deeds

Evil acts done in the furtherance of a malignancy, or in pursuit of forbidden lore, or done to appease a daemon always cause Corruption Points.

### Vile Persuasion

Many  Daemons  and  cult  leaders  are  masters  of  insidious temptation  and  of  sowing  the  seeds  of  doubt  in  a  faithful heart.  Their  words  and  arguments  can  corrupt  where  force alone would fail.

## The Malignancy Test

For every 10 Corruption Points a character gains, he must Test Willpower to see if his Corruption has manifested as literal [Damage](character-injury.md) to his body and soul. This roll is modified depending on the number of CP the character already possesses as noted on Table  10-7:  The  Corruption  Track .  If  the  Test  is failed, the character's Corruption of spirit is given form. These metaphysical and psychosomatic scars are called Malignancies, and are randomly rolled on Table 10-8: Malignancies . If a player rolls a result that he has previously suffered for failing a previous Malignancy Test, he must roll again.

## Mutation

A character's Corruption Points total is also used to determine the  warping  effects  of  Chaos  upon  his  body.  As  his Corruption builds, his flesh may revolt, twisted by the Dark Gods. Of course, such is the insidious nature of Chaos that it is constantly hunting for weakness in mind and body, testing a character's defences until  it  can  find  a  way  into  his  soul.  Forevery 30 Corruption Points a character gains, he must make a Test against two [Characteristics](starship-anatomy-detailed.md) of his choice or suffer a random Minor [Mutation](character-mutations-list.md) (see Chapter Xiv: Adversaries and Aliens ). He may not Test against the same [Characteristics](starship-anatomy-detailed.md) twice to resist [Mutation](character-mutations-list.md). A player should make a note on his Character Sheet of which Characteristics he has already Tested to resist mutation.

## Example

Ever ill [Fortune](chargen-stage2-origin-path.md)'s plaything, Rylar Mane discovers that the cloud-maidens of Chilautox are in fact part of a vile Chaos cult and falls afoul of one of their dark rituals. Infused with the power of [The Warp](warp-imperial-space-travel.md), Mane gains enough Corruption Points to raise his total to 31. This means he must make his first Malignancy Test by rolling against his Willpower. If he fails this Test then he must roll on the Malignancy Table and suffer the results. Should he continue his descent into corruption, Mane will need to test again when he reaches 61 and again at 91.

In  addition  to  his  Malignancy  Test,  reaching  30  or  more  CP  means  Mane  must  also  test  to  see  if  he  gains  a  [Mutation](character-mutations-list.md).  He  chooses  two [Characteristics](starship-anatomy-detailed.md), Strength and Fellowship, to roll against. If he fails either roll he will gain a [Mutation](character-mutations-list.md) (only one though, even if both tests are failed). Should he gain more CP later on, and have to take more mutation tests, he may never test against Strength of Fellowship again.
