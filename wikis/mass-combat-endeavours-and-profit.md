Players and GMs will notice right away that these new War  [Endeavours](economy-endeavours.md)  actually  cost  Profit  Factor.  This  is because waging a war, no matter what the [Size](character-traits.md), is always hideously expensive and an outcome of victory is never guaranteed. When players start a war they are wagering not  just  their  fortunes,  but  their  reputations  and  lives on the possibility of victory. If a Warfare Endeavour is successful, the players get their [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) investment back along with any profit factor [Rewards](economy-rewards.md) received. If the  war  fails  however,  not  only  are  the  Explorers  out their initial investment, they make no Profit Factor on the endeavour. War is a high-stakes game.

The  Profit  Factor  costs  and  benefits  of  [Warfare Endeavours](mass-combat-endeavours.md) vary widely because there are two potential levels  of  involvement that payers can have invested in them. Basically, fighting in someone else's war is always less costly than mounting your own, and thus fighting for someone else has less risks-and smaller rewards.
