T he Imperium runs as much on wealth and force of arms as it does favours, debts, and obligations. Throughout the dark corners of hive markets and orbital trading posts, citizens of the Imperium spend influence like currency-using it  to  prise  favours,  gifts,  and  Throne  Gelt  from  their  fellow man. Rogue Traders especially embrace this shadowy world of handshake deals and conditional promises. For the Explorers, it is a means to sway people to their cause or gain additional support  or  equipment  in  the  form  of  favours  by  using  the weight of their wealth and power to get their own way. [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) can be used in this fashion though Influence Tests. These Tests are contests against the [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) of those whom the Explorers  wish  to  influence,  with  success  granting  universal bonuses to [Interaction](rules-interaction.md) when dealing with the influenced party.

## Influence Tests

An Influence Test is a way for the Explorers to use their [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) to sway powerful organisations or gain favour from the wealthy and powerful of the Imperium. Naturally, the higher their [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) the more likely people are to take notice of them.

For every degree that the Explorers beat their opponent on this  Opposed  Test,  the  GM  can  grant  them  a  +5  on  all  [Interaction](rules-interaction.md) Skill Tests with the organisation or person for the remainder of their immediate dealings (the bonus lasts until the end of the current encounter or even longer at the GM's discretion). Alternatively, the GM can use an Influence Test as a one off roll to try to convince the person or organisation to grant the Explorers a favour, free passage, or give them some important information.

### Example

Lady  Fane  is  seeking  to  gain  the  favour  of  one  of  the  High  Houses of  Scintilla.  Approaching  representatives  of  the  house  with  her  own representatives, she begins a delicate social dance in an attempt to win favour. Lady Fane's [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) is 60, while that of the noble house she seeks to influence is 80. Making an Opposed T est, she rolls against her [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) and gets a 37, beating it by 23. Meanwhile, the GM rolls for the noble house, getting a 69, beating its value by 11. This means that Lady Fane has scored two degrees of success compared to the noble house's one degree of success-thus winning the Opposed Test by one degree and gaining a +5 on [Interaction](rules-interaction.md) Skill T ests for the remainder of her immediate business with the house.
