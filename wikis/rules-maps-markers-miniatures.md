These [Large-scale Warfare](mass-combat-rules.md) rules are designed to allow the GM to [Run](rules-combat-overview.md) large scale battles in a more abstract or narrative fashion using little more than a piece of paper and a pencil. That being said, they also lend themselves well to the use of tactical maps and miniatures. If the GM and players are so inclined, and want a little more detail in their game, they may utilise the guidelines for map and marker use as laid out in the Representing Combat Concretely or Abstractly sidebar on page 213 of the Rogue TRadeR core rulebook with a few adjustments. For large-scale ground warfare, each square on the tactical map, or each inch measured with a tape measure, represents one kilometre. It is important to note that multiple units can inhabit the same square on the tactical map. The GM should use common sense to determine just how many units can inhabit one square, and what, if any, modifiers should be applied to the units therein based on crowded, close-combat conditions.

LARGE-SCALE COMBAT SIMPLIFIED

While the above large-scale combat rules are highly detailed and allow the players and GM an incredible level of control over the outcome of their battle, they can be slightly time consuming. If the players and GM are unable or uninterested in using the given rules for large-scale combat, they may use [The Simple Method](mass-combats.md#the-simple-method) mass combat rules found on page 292 of the Rogue TRadeR core rulebook. This version encourages the GM to come up with a narrative for the combat, and have him run it as a narrative, with the Explorers making crucial contributions and vital Skill Tests at critical moments.
