These numbers are  generated  by  rolling  1d10  and  comparing  the  result  to the  chart  in Table 1-5: Starting Profit Factor and Ship Points .

The Game Master may generate (or simply choose!) the group's  starting  Profit  Factor  and  starship  himself  using Table 1-5 if he chooses, but another option is to have the players nominate one of their own to make the die roll. This method helps the players feel more involved in their group's [Campaign](rules-campaign.md).

## Profit Factor

The [Prestige](chargen-stage2-origin-path.md)  and  pedigree  of  a  Rogue  Trader's  Warrant  of Trade is measured in terms of raw profit and [Influence](economy-influence-rules.md). The [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) acquired from Table 1-5 is a representation of the relative value of the Warrant itself-what opportunities and resources it can call upon. See Profit  Factor on  page 270 for details.

### Starting Profit Factor

Most Rogue Trader Dynasties control vast amounts of capital, from hard currency like Thrones to large networks of agents, contacts,  and  investments  across  a  dozen  worlds.  These resources are all tied to the Rogue Trader's Warrant of Trade, the  priceless  document  that  grants  him  his  authority  and provides almost endless opportunities for success.

The total starting Profit Factor resulting from Table 1-5 represents the wealth that the Rogue Trader dynasty possesses due to its Warrant of Trade.

However,  the  Rogue  Trader's  accomplishments  are  not made alone. It takes strong allies, trusted friends, and wise counsellors to achieve the dizzying heights that most Rogue Traders aspire to. Certain choices on the Origin Path chart and other character creation options may add directly into the group's starting Profit.## Ship Points

At the heart of every Rogue Trader's operation is his vessel, a mighty starship with which he treads the void. Yet few ships are the same, even when crafted at the same dock or during the same era. Some Rogue Traders travel on patched-together [Transports](hulls-overview.md),  whilst  others  possess  mighty  [Frigates](hulls-overview.md)  or  cruisers bedecked with trophies and giltwork.

The total amount of Ship Points may be spent on either purchasing a customized ship or one of the quick-start vessels (see Chapter Viii: Starships for details on how to purchase a beginning ship with Ship Points). Any Ship Points that are not spent are added directly to the group's beginning [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) on a 1:1 basis.
