## Using Exploration Skills

[Exploration Skills](skills-descriptors.md) can help achieve a multitude of [Objectives](economy-endeavours.md), from prowling the remains of a derelict space hulk in search of  ancient  technology  to  surveying  a  planetary  surface  for rich mineral veins. [Exploration Skills](skills-descriptors.md) differ from Investigation Skills in that Exploration Skills tend to focus on searching or examining locations in order to find out what is there.

## Exploration Challenges

When a group of characters wishes to apply their collective specialities  to  an  exploration  task,  the  GM  can  create  an Exploration Challenge. This is simply an expanded version of a Skill Test where multiple characters can attempt to contribute to the success of the challenge over an extended period of time. To  create  an  Exploration  Challenge,  the  GM  considers  the nature and overall complexity of the challenge and assigns a total number of degrees of success necessary to complete the challenge. Table  9-27:  Exploration  Challenges provides some [Example](rules-tests.md) guidelines.

During an Exploration Challenge, each participating character can make a Skill Test using any Exploration Skill he possesses, though the GM may rule that certain Skills provide no meaningful help to task at hand. The same character can make multiple tests, but each time he must chose a different Exploration Skill. Likewise, the same Exploration Skill can be tested multiple times, but only if it is attempted by a different character each time. It is up to the players to decide the order of the Exploration Skill Tests.

The default difficulty of each individual Exploration Skill Test is Challenging (+0), but this can change depending on the outcome of previous tests made in the challenge. On a success, the difficulty of the next Exploration Skill Test has its difficulty reduced  by  one  step.  Furthermore,  each  degree  of  success contributes  an  equal  number  of  degrees  of  success  toward completing the Exploration Challenge. Conversely, a failure on an individual Exploration Skill Test increases the difficulty of the next Exploration Skill Test by one step. Furthermore, for each degree of failure remove one degree of success toward completing the Exploration Challenge.

Explorers  can  use  Fate  Points  to  affect  the  outcome  of Exploration Challenges if they wish.

### Example

Jericus and Mira have traced an unidentified signal to the surface of  a  barren  planet.  Due  to  magnetic  interference  affecting  their instruments, finding the signal's source will take considerable time. The GM rules this is a Taxing Exploration Challenge, requiring a total of six degrees of success to complete.

After some discussion, it is decided that Mira will attempt her test first, so she makes a Challenging (+0) Navigation (Surface) Test and gets a success (but no degrees of success). In this case, Mira's knowledge  of  Navigation  is  helpful  enough  to  make  the  next  test made in this challenge a little easier. Knowing this, Jericus decides to attempt an Ordinary (+10) Awareness Test. Jericus makes the test and gets two degrees of success.

The  group  now  needs  only  four  more  degrees  of  success  to complete the Exploration Challenge, plus Jericus's success also makes the next test in the challenge even less difficult. Now, Mira decides to make a Routine (+20) Awareness Test. Unfortunately, she makes a terrible roll and gets one degree of failure! Mira's hasty mistake costs the group one degree of success; furthermore, the next test made in this challenge will be a little more difficult.

Mira attempts to redeem herself with a Search Test of her own at a Difficulty of Ordinary (+10). She makes an excellent roll and gets four degrees of success! She then decides to spend a Fate Point to add one additional degree of success, allowing the group to complete the Exploration Challenge. The GM explains that Orlean notices some  distant  shapes.  The  group  originally  mistook  them  for  rock formations, however they are actually ancient ruins-clearly this is the source of the strange signal.

### Failing an Exploration Challenge

If the explorers collectively test all of their [Exploration Skills](skills-descriptors.md) without accumulating enough degrees of success to complete the  Exploration  Challenge,  they  fail  the  challenge  and  are delayed. The exact consequences of such a [Delay](rules-combat-overview.md) are largely up to the GM, but the explorers should never be faced with a  permanent,  insurmountable  failure.  Instead,  the  explorers could be forced to return their ship or base of operations and formulate a new strategy, or they could be temporarily stuck in a dangerous environment where their very survival is at stake. Whatever the consequences, the GM should eventually give them another chance to tackle the Exploration Challenge.

## Using Investigation Skills

In  many cases,  exploration  begins  with  a  lead,  clue,  or  tip that  requires  further  investigation  or  research  before  the information can be trusted. Expeditions Into the Unknown are dangerous business, and the wise Rogue Trader does all he can to mitigate the risks. Investigation techniques might include tracking down key individuals and extracting informationwhether  through  [Charm](equipment-gear.md),  intimidation,  or  bargaining-or using personal contacts to uncover data on a famous figure in the history of the Imperium. Other methods of investigation could  involve  canvassing  the  local  populace  for  rumours, translating  ancient  tablets,  or  retracing  the  path  of  a  failed expedition. Ultimately, a successful bit of investigating comes down to knowing where to look, who to talk to, and where to go once the information has been obtained. [Investigation Skills](skills-descriptors.md) differ from [Exploration Skills](skills-descriptors.md) in that Investigation Skills tend  to  focus  on  answering  questions,  or  seeking  specific information, without knowing where to look.

All  Investigation  Skill  Tests  require  a  character  to  have  access to an Investigation Skill. The difficulty of the test depends on the complexity of the task, which also determines the base time it should take to get a [Reliable](weapons-general.md) answer. A success on the test allows the character to roll 1d10 and add the appropriate Characteristic  bonus  (as  determined  by  the  Investigation Skill's  governing  characteristic)  and  subtract  the  value  in hours from the time it takes to uncover the information. A failure  indicated  no  progress  is  gained.  Failing  by  three  or more degrees indicates a setback, adding 1d10 hours to the total time required by the test. Failing by five or more degrees means the investigation has come to a complete halt and any further investigation on the topic has its difficulty increased by  two  steps.  After  the  investigating  time  has  elapsed,  the investigating character gets the information he was seeking.

### Hastily Conducted Investigations

The  investigation  benchmarks  described  above  assume  that characters conducting an investigation desire to acquire information that is as complete and [Reliable](weapons-general.md) as possible and are willing to invest as much time as necessary to achieve that goal. Of course, there are bound to be times when characters would like to conduct a complex investigation, but simply don't have enough time to conduct it thoroughly no matter how many lucky  breaks  come  their  way .  In  such  cases,  characters  may simply  want  to  get  the  best  information  they  can  within  a limited time frame. This type of hasty investigation tends to either return very few details, or a great deal of information that is contradictory, potentially misleading, incomprehensible, or simply irrelevant. Nevertheless, sometimes that is the best the investigators can hope for.

When  conducting  a  hasty  investigation,  a  character  first decides  how  much  he  is  willing  to  devote  to  the  task,  and then  he  makes  an  Investigation  Skill  Test  as  normal.  If  the test is successful, he gets some information, but its reliability is  suspect-the  GM  can  consult Table  9-29:  Reliability of Hastily Gathered Information for  some  guidelines for adjusting the results of a complex investigation performed in much less than the recommended time.
