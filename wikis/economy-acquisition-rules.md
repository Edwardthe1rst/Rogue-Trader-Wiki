## Acquisition Tests

To  succeed  in  an  Acquisition  Test,  the  Explorer  must  roll equal to or less than his [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) on 1d100. This roll is modified depending on the [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md), [Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md), and Scale of the acquisition:

-  If  the  roll  is  equal  to  or  less  than  the  Explorer's [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md), then he has acquired the item.
-  If  the  roll  is  greater  than  the  Explorer's  Profit  Factor, then  he  has  not  acquired  the  item  and  it  remains  out of  reach  or  beyond  his  means  for  the  time  being  (see Frequency of Acquisition  for  details  on  retrying  failed Acquisition Tests).

[Availability](economy-availability-rules.md) will either be set by the GM or can be found in Chapter V: Armoury , while [Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md) and Scale are usually chosen by the Explorer.

## A Matter of Scale

Most items and resources can be obtained in varying amounts and  sizes-a  group  of  mercenaries  could  be  hired  as  as  a squad, company, or regiment, for [Example](rules-tests.md). There are seven Scale categories ranging from Negligible to Vast. The effects of Scale on Acquisition Tests can be found on Table 9-35: Acquisition Modifiers .

## Acquisition as a Plot Device

A failed Acquisition Test does not need to mean that the item is completely out of reach of the Explorers. The GM can work such events into the plot-perhaps the item the PCs were trying to acquire has just been bought  by  a  [Rival](talents-descriptions.md)  Rogue  Trader  and  they  must  try to  steal  it  back,  or  maybe  they  received  what  they believed  were  genuine  goods  only  to  discover  them flawed or fake and they must try to track down the dodgy merchant they bought the goods from to get the real merchandise.

The GM may require the Explorer to spend time seeking out the item or waiting for it to be constructed or trained before he can make an Acquisition Test. In this case, refer to the rules for [Availability and Time](economy-availability-rules.md) on page 111.

### Example

Master-at-arms  Herodor  is  looking  for  a  new  sidearm  after  his previous  one  was  devoured  by  a  Dusk  Stalker.  Looking  for  both reliability and power, he selects a Lucius Pattern Hellpistol (Rare [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md)  -10).  Herodor is  also  looking  for  something  worthy of his rank and so chooses to seek out an above average [Example](rules-tests.md) of the firearm (Good [Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md) -10). Since he only wants a single pistol, this is considered a very small cost for his dynasty (Negligible Scale  +30).  Adding  these  together  gives  him  a  total  Acquisition Modifier of +10. Herodor's [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) is 40, so he must roll 50 or less to acquire his pistol.

## Combining Acquisitions

Some items can be upgraded or modified to combine two or more items together, such as a [Laspistol](weapons-general.md) with a [Red-dot Laser Sight](weapons-upgrades.md). In this case, when testing to acquire the combined item, compare the [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md)  of  the  item's  [Components](starship-anatomy-detailed.md)  and  use the greatest penalty to determine a base [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md) Modifier. Each additional component then results in an additional -5 penalty to the base Availability Modifier.

### Example

Herodor  wants  to  acquire  an  [Autogun](weapons-general.md)  with  a  fire  selector.  To work  out  this  weapon's  [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md)  Modifier,  he  first  finds  the [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md)  of  the  [Autogun](weapons-general.md)  (Average  +10)  and  then  compares  it to  the  Availability  of  the  fire  selector  (Scarce  +0).  Since  the  [Fire Selector](weapons-upgrades.md)  has  the  higher  Availability  (Scarce  +0),  this  becomes  the base Availability Modifier. This base is then modified by -5 for each addition component beyond the first. In this case, the autogun has one additional component (the [Fire Selector](weapons-upgrades.md)), giving the acquisition a final Availability Modifier of -5.## Automatic Success and Failure

Some items are out of the reach of the Explorers, while others are  ridiculously  easy  for  them  to  acquire  given  the  wealth and resources of their dynasty. In these instances, no die roll is made.

-  If  bonuses to the Explorer's Acquisition Test ever increases his [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) to 100 or more before rolling, then the Test is automatically passed.
-  If penalties to the Explorer's Acquisition Test ever reduces his [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) to 0 or less before rolling, then the Test is automatically failed.

### Example

Herodor wishes to acquire a [Stub Automatic](weapons-general.md) for himself after a mishap involving decompression and his prized hellpistol. This is an easy weapon to find (Plentiful +30) and he is settling for an off the rack version (Common +0). He also only requires one (Negligible +30). This gives a modifier of +60, which when added to his [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) of 40 increases it to 100. As he cannot fail the Acquisition Test, he automatically acquires this fine [Example](rules-tests.md) of Imperial weaponry.

## Acquisition and Starting Characters

When a player creates a new character, he may make a single 'free'  acquisition  to  represent  personal  equipment  that  has been collected painstakingly over time or perhaps gifted to the character by the dynasty. Starting characters may therefore be equipped using the following guidelines:

-  They may choose a single item with a total Acquisition Modifier of +0 or more without the need to make an Acquisition Test.
-  In  the  case  of  [Weapons](weapons-general.md),  a  character  may  only  choose those which he can use. i.e., he must have a corresponding Weapon Training Talent.

As  always,  the  GM  has  the  final  say  in  which  personal acquisitions a player may select in this way-possibly limiting certain [Weapons](weapons-general.md), [Armour](armour.md), or [Gear](equipment-gear.md) based on his own setting for the game or for the purposes of the plot.

## Unscrupulous Traders

Acquisition  Modifiers  assume  that  the  merchant  or trader  that  the  Explorers  are  dealing  with  is  giving them a fair price based on the item's true value. This is not always the case, and in some instances a trader may pass on goods at greatly increased-or in some rare cases reduced-prices. The GM can represent this by changing the total Acquisition Modifier by between -30 to +30 points to give a final altered modifier. For their  part,  Explorers  can  use  the  Evaluate  Skill  (see page 81) to gauge an item's true Acquisition Modifier.

## Frequency of Acquisition

It is up to the GM as to when and how often the Explorers may make Acquisition Tests to acquire goods. As a general rule, he should restrict acquisition to appropriate junctures (such as visits to mercantile districts, time at port, and when dealing with local traders) and to downtime between play such as at the start or end of a gaming session. For important or large acquisitions,  the  GM  may  also  require  the  completion  of  a series of in game conditions such as finding a specific seller or travelling to a certain locale where the items can be procured.

### Example

Lady Fane is seeking  to  equip  her  personal  regimental  bodyguard with  artificer  carapace  [Armour](armour.md).  The  GM  decides  that  before  her player can make an Acquisition Test Lady Fane must travel to the remote [Forge World](chargen-stage2-origin-path.md) of Scranthos-the only place in the sector where this kind of [Armour](armour.md) is manufactured. Once there, the Lady will need to speak at length with the local tech-priests, make observances to the Machine God, and perhaps even offer future promises of aid and support. Only then can the true mercantile wrangling begin and an Acquisition Test be made.Acquisition is a tool for the GM to map the aspiration of the Explorers and measure their mercantile power, and it should not become a stumbling block every time an Explorer wants to find a new suit of flak armour. Therefore, the GM should relax  these  rules  for  personal  items,  especially  those  with Average [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md) (or more) and Common [Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md) (or less). Explorers should be able to buy personal [Weapons](weapons-general.md), armour, and [Gear](equipment-gear.md) as often as they like-provided they have access to the goods they seek and the time to acquire them.

If  an  Explorer  fails  an  Acquisition  Test,  then  it  is  up  to the  GM  to  decide  when  he  may  make  it  again-taking into  account  access  to  resources,  Merchants,  and  markets. Typically, however, the Explorer should have to spend time looking for new vendors or traders or possibly travelling to a new location before attempting to acquire the same kind of item. By the same token, if one Explorer in the group fails to acquire an item during a mercantile foray, it stands to reason that attempts by other Explorers will also meet with failureif an item is not available, then it is not available.

### Example

Lady Fane has failed to acquire the suits of artificer carapace [Armour](armour.md) from  the  forge  world  of  Scranthos.  The  GM,  therefore,  decides that she can either travel farther afield, or she can drop anchor at Scranthos for a month or two and see if prospects improve, possibly causing her outstanding contracts or [Endeavours](economy-endeavours.md) to suffer. The GM might even decide that the tech-priests would be more than willing to  renegotiate-provided of course the Lady takes care of a little problem for them…

## Commerce and Acquisition

In addition to using his dynasty's raw wealth, an Explorer can also employ personal Skills to try to improve prices, outwit traders,  and  ease  mercantile  transactions.  An  Explorer  may use the Commerce Skill (see page 79) to improve his [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) prior to making an Acquisition Test. This is a contest of the Explorer's Commerce Skill versus the Commerce Skill or Scrutiny Skill of the person or organisation he is trading with. For each degree the Explorer beats his opponent, he may increase his [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) by 2 points. For each degree his opponent beats him, however, he must decrease his Profit Factor  by  2.  Both  increases  and  decreases  to  Profit  Factor only  apply  to  a  single  Acquisition  Test  made  immediately with that trader.

This use of Skills works both ways, and when the Explorers deal with canny Merchants, the GM may force them to make a contest of Commerce Skills even if they don't want to.

## Ammo, Drugs, and Grenades

Many items that an Explorer owns are disposable, such  as  special  [Ammunition](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md),  drugs,  and  grenades. Once an Explorer has acquired such an item, then he is considered to have a steady supply of it and can take as much or as little of it as he wishes (restricted only by weight and common sense) when he leaves his vessel. Then when he returns to his vessel, he can replenish his supply. So for [Example](rules-tests.md), if an Explorer has purchased [Man-stopper Bullets](weapons-ammunition.md), he has a constant supply that he can  top  off  with  whenever  he  returns  to  base.  This assumption  also  applies  to  standard  [Ammunition](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md)  for [Weapons](weapons-general.md) as well.

If the Explorers don't want to keep track of every round  of  ammo  or  exactly  which  consumables  they are carrying, the GM can use the Rule of Three. This simply means that each Explorer will have three reloads for each of their [Weapons](weapons-general.md), three grenades of each type they carry, three doses of their chosen drugs, and so forth for the current situation until they can resupply.

Finally,  the  GM  can  also  choose  to  disrupt  the flow of disposable items, either to reflect events in the Explorers' locale or if he feels they might be using too much of a particular  resource  and  thus  depleting  its supply. This disruption can be most easily achieved by using the rules for [Availability and Time](economy-availability-rules.md), forcing the Explorers to find shipments of goods and then wait for them to arrive.

### Example

Lady  Fane  is  now  looking  to  acquire  a  quiet  retreat  in  the  Gas Clouds of Webrune. Tracking down the owner of an abandoned observation  platform,  she  begins  haggling  over  the  price.  Rolling against Lady Fane's Commerce Skill, her player fails the roll by 10. However, the owner does even worse and fails his roll by 27. These results mean that Lady Fane has beaten him by one degree of success and increases her [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) by 2 for the following Acquisition Test.

The GM may also allow the use of the Command, [Charm](equipment-gear.md), or  Barter  Skills  to  be  used  in  place  of  Commerce  should the situation seem appropriate. These should be used rarely, however, and always specific to the person or organisation being traded with.

### Example

Herodor has alighted on the shrine world of Pryticus where he is looking to acquire medical supplies for his vessel. The dour monks of this grey world have little interest in money or wealth, so if he wishes to improve his chances of gaining his supplies he must try to convince them of his devotion to the Emperor. The GM decides that to convince the monks of his piety, Herodor must use the [Charm](equipment-gear.md) Skill instead of Commerce-time for Herodor to sing the praises of the God-Emperor.## Acquisition of Starship [Components](starship-anatomy-detailed.md)

Few things acquired or maintained by the Explorers' dynasty compare to the astronomical value of their void-ships. When the  Explorers  wish  to  acquire  new  [Components](starship-anatomy-detailed.md)  for  their vessel,  or  commandeer  a  new  vessel,  the  GM  should  work out the Acquisition Modifier as normal using [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md) and [Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md)  (Availabilities  for  Starship  Components  can be found in Chapter VIII:Starships ). However, in place of the Scale Acquisition Modifier, he should use the modifiers detailed in T able 9-36: Starship Component Acquisitions based on the kind of system being acquired.

### Example

[Lord-captain](rank-lord-captain.md) Brevarn of the Armsmaiden wishes to refit the great fore  macrocannons  Primus  and  Secondus-upgrading  them  from Common [Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md) (+0) to Good (-10). As a War component, an additional modifier of -30 is added, giving a final Acquisition Modifier of -40.

## Acquiring a Starship

In rare cases, the Explorers may even wish to acquire a whole starship rather than just starship [Components](starship-anatomy-detailed.md). To find the base Acquisition Modifier for a new vessel, the modifier is based on the [Size](character-traits.md) of its [Hull](starship-anatomy-detailed.md). A ship's Hull Modifier is equal to the hull's worth in Ship Points (see Chapter VIII: Startships for details on [Hulls](hulls-overview.md) and their Ship Point value). Because starships are extremely rare and powerful, only one warp-capable vessel may be acquired at a time. In addition, no additional modifiers aside from those gained by a successful Commerce Test may assist with Acquisition Tests to acquire a starship-the fact that a Rogue Trader 'is only buying one!' does not make things any easier.

It is important to note that building a brand-new starship is an exacting task requiring many years (often decades) and much toil. Normally, building new starships is reserved to The Imperial Navy. Rogue Traders are far more likely to acquire a  recomissioned  Naval  vessel  or  repair  and  refit  a  salvaged derelict.

## Unusual Acquisitions

Chapter V: Armoury covers the [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md) and [Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md)  of  equipment  and  retainers,  while Chapter Viii: Starships covers Starships and their [Components](starship-anatomy-detailed.md), but these represent only a small fraction of the potential resources that the Explorers can acquire. A resource can be pretty much anything the GM can imagine and the Explorers place value on.  Most  resources  provide  a  tangible  benefit,  such  as  the favour of the High Born of Scintilla or landing rights to the Drusian Shrine Worlds. However, some can simply be trophies that the Explorers are willing to pay for-like artefacts of the Astartes or the preserved remains of a perished xeno species. The GM should set an acquisition's [Availability](economy-availability-rules.md) based on its rarity using Table 9-38: Acquisition Rarity as a guide.

Finally the GM can assign a Scale to the acquisition using A Matter of Scale (see page 271) as a guide.

## Upkeep Tests

Even after the Explorers have acquired an item, it may still be a drain on the resources of the dynasty-requiring [Fuel](weapons-ammunition.md) to [Run](rules-combat-overview.md), dues to be paid, or careful maintenance and repair. At certain times (see below), the GM may require the Explorers to make an Upkeep Test to see if they lose their acquisition or it is diminished or degraded in some way . An Upkeep Test functions in the same way as an Acquisition Test, using all the same modifiers and factors involved in acquiring the original item-in effect, the Explorers are acquiring the item or resource again. If they pass the Test, then their acquisition is unaffected, otherwise they have encountered a shortfall in funds or a waning of favour or [Influence](economy-influence-rules.md) and must choose one of the following options:

- Discard the item or resource: · The Explorers cannot use the  acquisition  until  it  is  repaired,  reloaded,  or  purchased anew. An acquisition lost in this way cannot be reacquired until the Explorers' [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) increases.
- Downgrade  the  item  or  resource: · The  acquisition's [Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md) is reduced by one level (i.e., Best to Good, Good to Common, etc.). If the acquisition is already of Poor

[Craftsmanship](components-craftsmanship.md), then this option may not be selected.

- Downsize the item or resource: · The acquisition's Scale is  reduced by one level (i.e., Major to Standard, Minor to Trivial, etc.). If the acquisition is already of Negligible Scale, then this option may not be selected.
- Overstretch  Profit  Factor: · The  Explorers  incur  a  -5 penalty  to  all  Acquisition  Tests  until  either  the  Explorers' [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md) increases or they choose to discard, downgrade, or downsize the acquisition.

### Example

Lady Fane has [Run](rules-combat-overview.md) into some financial difficulties concerning her mansion in the Grace system. The GM requires Lady Fane's player to make an Upkeep Test to maintain the property, which given its high expense she fails. The player now has a choice of whether to discard the property, downgrade-allowing it to fall into disrepair, downsize-selling off some of the land, or overstretching her [Profit Factor](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md).

## When to Make an Upkeep Test

The  GM  can  require  the  Explorers  to  make  Upkeep  Tests whenever they suffer a reduction in their fortunes or commit to a massive expenditure of wealth. He can also call on them to make a Test when an item is at risk of [Damage](character-injury.md) or destruction. Some examples of when the GM can require the Explorers to make an Upkeep Test include:

- When  an  acquisition  is  expended,  damaged, · or destroyed: Such as a suit of [Armour](armour.md) worn by an  Explorer  that  suffers  massive  [Damage](character-injury.md),  a planetary Settlement bombarded from orbit, or  a  supply  of  rare  [Ammunition](economy-wealth-and-acquisitions.md)  usedexcessively .

- When  a  [Rival](talents-descriptions.md)  or  adversary  attacks  the  Explorers' · holdings or tries to steal their goods or retainers: Such as a mass kidnapping of crewmen on shore leave or when a  treacherous  [Adept](rules-allies-enemies-rivals.md)  is  skimming  off  their  profits  for  ship repairs, leading to the forfeiting of valuable [Components](starship-anatomy-detailed.md).
- When fate  plays  a  hand  in  the  Explorers'  dynasty: · Such  as  when  a  hired  mercenary  company  with  divided loyalties gets a better offer or there is a system-wide plague that threatens the Explorers' local promethium suppliers.

Ultimately , it is up to the GM to decide when the Explorers should make Upkeep Tests, and he should use them as a tool to both remind the PCs that their worldly possession can be tenuous and that there are often consequences for their actions.

### Example

Herodor sends a company of armsmen into battle during a vicious boarding action in the deeps of the Koronus Passage. Because of the ferocity of the fighting, the GM decides that once the battle is over the Explorer will need to make an Upkeep Test to see what is left of his men.
