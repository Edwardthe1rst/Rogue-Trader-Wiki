### Types of Names

There are five types of name presented here:

[Primitive](weapons-general.md) names are short and brutal-often like the lives of their owners.

Low Gothic names trace their Lineage to a High Gothic past, but have become much debased with centuries of use.

High  Gothic names  are  drawn  from  the  ancient  High Gothic tongue. They have an [Air of Authority](talents-descriptions.md) and seriousness.

Archaic names herald from the distant past. They might be the names of mighty saints or heroes from Ministorum legends. Others are so ancient they have lost all trace of their origins.

Informal names are just that-nicknames, terms and slang names that a person might earn through deeds (or misdeeds).

You may decide that a particular type of name is especially appropriate  for  your  character.  If  this  is  the  case,  you  could simply choose it from the appropriate lists below. Otherwise roll randomly.

The character creation process provides you with the skeleton of your alter ego; however, these numbers and notes are not the sum total of your character-there is of course a vital fifth element: you! The background details of your character's life are largely up to you as is, of course, their personality.

Some people like to develop their characters during play, and that is a perfectly reasonable approach. When you start to play, it may be enough to know that your character is a [Death World](chargen-stage2-origin-path.md) [Arch-militant](career-arch-militant.md) who has left Port Wander to lead a renowned Rogue Trader's bodyguard company. Many players, however,  prefer  to  work  out  background  and  personality before play begins. If you need some help fleshing out your character,  try  answering  these  six  questions.  They  should allow  you  to  focus  your  thoughts  on  your  emerging  alter ego.

### What Is Your Demeanour?

Perhaps the first thing to consider is your personality. What are  you  like?  Fiery  and  passionate  or  earthy  and  practical? Are  you  a  perpetual  pessimist,  or  do  you  always  believe the  best  will  happen?  Do  you  seek  out  people  or  are  you a misanthrope? What is your instinctive response to threats, surprises,  or  pressure  to  perform?  Here  are  some  [Traits](character-traits.md)  that might get you started:

Bilious: Suspicious,  bitter,  and  angry,  yet  very  watchful and aware.

Cardinal: Seeks  careful  balance  in  all  things,  yet  often struggles to make a vital choice rapidly.

Choleric: Always  active  and  taking  [Charge](rules-combat-overview.md),  yet  easy  to provoke to anger.

Fixed: Solid,  unyielding,  and  definitive  in  all  things. Considered and settled of opinion.

Melancholic: Thoughtful, introspective, and [Prone](combat-special-circumstances.md) to fits of gloom.

Mutable: Unpredictable,  undisciplined,  and  rebellious, defined by opposition to ideals.

Phlegmatic: Practical  and  careful,  yet  very  serious  and reserved.

Sanguine: Confident and optimistic, but [Prone](combat-special-circumstances.md) to visions and flights of whimsy.

Supine: Loyal, devout, and easily led.

### Why Are You a Leader Aboard a Rogue Trader Vessel?

At some time in your past, your life took a turn unlike almost all others. Now you sit in the [Lord-captain](rank-lord-captain.md)'s throne aboard a mighty void-ship, or perhaps stand at the Lord-captain's right hand to advise him. Is this mere chance? Did the God-Emperor gaze upon you with favour and move the workings of men and  worlds  such  that  an  unlikely  meeting  blossomed into  this  present  reality?  Or  perhaps  you  yourself laboured with purpose and for years to engineer your  present  situation.  If  so,  how  is  it  that you  succeeded  where  countless  men  fail?

Perhaps you were lifted from obscurity by a Rogue Trader's fleeting whim and given the chance to prove yourself. Perhaps you worked your way up from the lowest of crew by toil and violence. Perhaps your talents attracted the attention of your betters during trade negotiations, and your fealty and service were made a part of the final arrangement. It is also plausible that you have a strong interest in the Rogue Trader's mission: maybe there are things in The Halo Stars that you seek out, and the Rogue Trader is your ally in this endeavour. Perhaps your wealth has purchased your present position of [Influence](economy-influence-rules.md) aboard this vessel. Or maybe you are fleeing a dark fate that lies behind you in the Imperium, and your present position is the greatest to which you can ascend under the circumstances. Whatever the circumstances of your recent past, they will likely colour your opinions and beliefs for some time to come.

### Why Does the Koronus Expanse Call to You?

The Koronus Expanse region of The Halo Stars beyond The Calixis Sector is a place of dread rumour, death, and wealth beyond measure awaiting those brave enough to claim it. It is the rock upon which dynasties of Rogue Traders have been dashed to death, and it is the spire that has brought others into the shining light of Imperial glory. The Expanse is a realm of treasure, risk, secrets, and vile xenos, fated to be scourged and claimed in the God-Emperor's name-or so say the Dissident Drusians. Why have you toiled to leave the protections of the Imperium and to venture through the dire storm-voids into the terrible, wondrous realm of The Halo Stars? Is it your lust for  treasure  and  the  life  that  can  be  bought  with  artefacts [From Beyond](character-traits.md)? Do you see it as your Emperor-given duty to aid the expansion of Imperial frontiers? Are you running from some  secret  that  will  not  follow  you  beyond  the  reach  of Imperial powers, or are you pursuing [Vengeance](chargen-stage2-origin-path.md) against one who has already fled to the Halo Stars? Perhaps you chase a  dark  trail  of  secrets  and  forbidden  lore  that  lead  you  to the Dead Worlds of the Expanse, or perhaps the expansion of Imperial knowledge itself is your goal.

### What Will You Sacrifice?

All too many people shy from their final victories, scared by what it will cost to achieve their dreams. How deep are the [Wounds](character-injury.md) you are willing to suffer on the path to wealth, fame, revenge, or forbidden lore? How far will you go and how many of your dearly held beliefs will die along the way? Are you willing to sacrifice health, limbs, or your very sanity if the quest requires it? Will you use your crew, your allies, or even your companions as mere stepping stones, trading their lives for advantage as the game comes to a close and victory beckons? Are you willing to step from the path of the faithful and strike deals with xenos, vile psykers, and darker powers to attain your ends at the cost of your immortal soul? These choices are yours: what are you willing to sacrifice for victory, and how will you live with the consequences?### What Is Your Ambition?

Ambition  [Drives](components-drives.md)  Rogue  Traders  and  their  allies:  the  raw urge  for  power,  for  wealth,  or  to  serve  the  God-Emperor of Mankind in ways that will echo down through the ages. What  is  your  ambition?  Perhaps  it  is  to  drive  your  [Sword](weapons-general.md) into the map of stars and see your name writ there next to worlds claimed in the Emperor's name. Perhaps you desire [Renown](chargen-stage2-origin-path.md) to match the Imperial heroes of legend, or perhaps you wish to reclaim all the lost human souls of The Halo Stars and  become  a  High  Ecclesiarch  of  a  new  Imperial  realm. Maybe your ambitions are less lofty, but no less noble-to be acknowledged a master of war by an Imperial Lord-general, to regain your lost faith in the Imperial Creed through some grand act of penitence, or to discover lost medicae-tech that will erase your [Mutations](character-mutations-list.md). Ambition is as varied as humanity, and you are defined by what drives you to great acts.

### What Are Your Hatreds?

The burning [Flame](weapons-general.md) of [Hatred](talents-descriptions.md) is an Imperial virtue: it sustains men against  terror,  xenos,  rivalry,  and  defeat  even  when  it burns cold and slow. What hatreds sustain you in dark days and drive you to acts of destruction or penitence? Do you loathe  the  mutant  or  the  pirate  who  prays  to  Dark  Gods? Do you wish a fiery doom upon decadent nobles, or desire to murder the low-deck [Scum](rules-allies-enemies-rivals.md) of your vessel with death-gas? Do you wish to lay low the vile Ork or treacherous Eldar? Have the inhabitants of a specific Imperial world, a renowned Ministorum Cult, or a [Rival](talents-descriptions.md) Rogue Trader's crew earned your lasting wrath? Maybe you simply cannot stand to be shown wrong, or to be in the presence of those who speak falsely, or to be seen to fail at certain tasks.
