Once  elements  of  one  unit  are  within  one  kilometre  of elements  of  another,  they  are  considered  in  contact  and [Combat](rules-combat-overview.md)  may  begin.  This  doesn't  mean  that  all  combatants are within a one kilometre area, or even that all troops are engaged. It simply means that the units are close enough to see one another and deal [Damage](character-injury.md), and that combat may now be joined in earnest. As stated earlier, when units meet there is always the possibility of [Surprise](combat-surprise-rules.md) if the GM sees fit.

While  this  holds  true  of  'direct  fire'  units  like  [Armour](armour.md)  and infantry ,  artillery  and  air  forces  operate  in  a  slightly  different manner. 'Indirect fire' units, artillery and air forces, can [Attack](combat-attack-rules.md) from over  the  horizon  and  have  a  range  characteristic  listed  in  their [Description](career-path-format-guide.md) that reflects how far they can reach. For [Example](rules-tests.md), a medium artillery unit has a range of 20 kilometres, meaning that it is in contact with another unit when it is within 20 kilometres. These units can attack foes and not be attacked in turn.

*Source:* `Battle Fleet of the Koronus, page 134`
