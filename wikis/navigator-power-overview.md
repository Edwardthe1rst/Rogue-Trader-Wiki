In Rogue Trader Navigator characters have access to a  range  of  powers,  many  only  vaguely  understood outside the halls of The Great Houses. These powers can  allow  a  Navigator  to  do  many  things,  ranging from glimpsing into the future to perceiving the very flow  of  the  warp  itself.  Unlike  a  Psyker  however,  a Navigator's command of [The Warp](warp-imperial-space-travel.md) is more innate and instinctive and so he does not manifest his abilities in the same way. To this end [Navigators](psychic-psyker-types.md) have a far more limited range of abilities than Psykers but their powers are more [Reliable](weapons-general.md) and in some cases more potent. Like a  Psyker  though,  a  Navigator  must  choose  which abilities he wishes to pursue. It is rare that a Navigator will ever master all of his powers and realise his full potential, though it is more than possible to become a master of a single ability.

Navigator characters also suffer [Mutation](character-mutations-list.md) more than any other character-save any that succumb to the lure of the dark gods-and as their power grows they will typically become slowly more and more corrupted in body and mind by both the debilitating nature of [The Warp](warp-imperial-space-travel.md) and the ongoing effect of the Navigator genecoding which is both their curse and their blessing.
