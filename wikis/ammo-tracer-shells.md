Flame weapons use liquid fuel, commonly promethium. When  promethium  is laced with encapsulated nephium motes, a potent secondary ignition takes place a few seconds after the promethium is lit. Few groups make use of this knowledge, for  it  greatly  increases  the  chance  of  accidental  ignition  of flamer fuel stores, requiring expensive safeguards.
